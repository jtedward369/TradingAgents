# Fundamental Analysis Report: Amazon.com, Inc. (AMZN)
**Date of Analysis:** April 12, 2026
**Sector:** Consumer Cyclical | **Industry:** Internet Retail

---

## 1. Executive Summary
Amazon.com, Inc. (AMZN) demonstrates robust financial health characterized by accelerating revenue growth, significant improvements in net income, and substantial capital investment in its infrastructure. After a period of volatility in 2022, the company has transitioned into a high-growth, high-profitability phase. With a market capitalization exceeding $2.5 trillion, Amazon continues to leverage its scale to expand margins and cash flow generation.

## 2. Company Profile & Business Overview
Amazon is a global leader in e-commerce, cloud computing (AWS), digital advertising, and streaming services. Its business model relies on a massive logistics network and a high-margin cloud segment to fuel reinvestment into retail and emerging technologies. The company's recent financial trajectory shows a strong shift from pure revenue growth to significant bottom-line profitability.

## 3. Financial Performance Analysis

### A. Income Statement Analysis (Profitability & Growth)
*   **Revenue Growth:** Total revenue has seen a consistent upward trajectory, growing from approximately $514 billion in 2022 to $717 billion in 2025 (TTM). This indicates successful expansion of market share and service offerings.
*   **Net Income Turnaround:** After reporting a net loss in 2022 (-$2.7B), Amazon has achieved explosive net income growth, reaching $77.7B in 2025. This reflects optimized operational efficiency and higher-margin revenue streams (likely AWS and Advertising).
*   **Operating Efficiency:** Operating income has grown from $12.2B in 2022 to $79.9B in 2025, showing a strong ability to scale operations while controlling costs.

### B. Balance Sheet Analysis (Liquidity & Solvency)
*   **Asset Expansion:** Total assets grew from $463B in 2022 to $818B in 2025, driven largely by massive investments in Property, Plant, and Equipment (Net PPE) and other non-current assets.
*   **Equity & Retained Earnings:** Common Stock Equity has increased significantly, reaching $411B in 2025, up from $146B in 2022. Retained earnings also show a healthy upward trend, providing a substantial buffer for future growth.
*   **Debt Profile:** While total debt has increased to $153B, it is well-supported by the company's massive asset base and growing equity. The debt-to-equity ratio (approx. 43.4%) remains manageable for a company of this scale.

### C. Cash Flow Analysis (Cash Generation & Reinvestment)
*   **Operating Cash Flow:** Operating cash flow has seen a massive surge, reaching $139.5B in 2025. This provides the "fuel" for all other activities.
*   **Capital Expenditure (CapEx):** Amazon remains in a heavy investment phase. Capital expenditures reached $131.8B in 2025. While this significantly impacts free cash flow in the short term, it is essential for maintaining its competitive moat in logistics and cloud infrastructure.
*   **Free Cash Flow (FCF):** Despite heavy CapEx, the company maintains a positive FCF of ~$7.7B (TTM), though it has fluctuated due to the aggressive reinvestment cycle.

## 5. Actionable Insights & Trader Recommendations

### **For Long-Term Investors (Bulls)**
*   **Growth Engine:** The transition from a net loss in 2022 to nearly $80B in annual net income demonstrates a mature yet growing business. The "scale effect" is clearly working.
*   **Infrastructure Moat:** The heavy CapEx is not "waste" but an investment in the physical and digital infrastructure that competitors struggle to replicate.
*   **Action:** **BUY/HOLD**. Amazon is a core growth holding.

### **For Short-Term Traders (Volatility Play)**
*   **Beta & Momentum:** With a Beta of 1.38, the stock is more volatile than the broader market. Traders should look for entries near the 200-day moving average ($224.86) if the price dips.
*   **Valuation Sensitivity:** The PE ratio of 33 suggests the stock is sensitive to interest rate changes and earnings misses.
*   **Action:** **WATCH**. Monitor for support levels around the 50-day average ($213.40).

## 6. Conclusion
Amazon is a powerhouse of fundamental growth. The company has successfully navigated the post-pandemic shifts, turning massive scale into massive profit. While the high CapEx requirements and valuation multiples require careful monitoring, the underlying strength of the balance sheet and the growth in operating cash flow provide a highly favorable outlook for investors.
